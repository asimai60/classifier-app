Introduction

In this project we build an application using traditional computer vision techniques to accurately distinguish between plastic and glass bottles. The system employs a combination of image preprocessing, edge detection, circular segmentation, and feature-based classification to achieve this goal. The intended applications include personal use, automated recycling systems, quality control in manufacturing, and waste management.

Approach and Method

Our project employs a two-stage classification system to accurately identify the material of bottle bottoms—either glass or plastic. This method was developed after initial attempts using full-bottle imaging proved challenging due to the translucency and diverse shapes of bottles.

Effective Techniques and Findings:

  • Segmentation:
    • We refined image quality through preprocessing—resizing, grayscaling, and applying Gaussian blurring.
    • Utilizing the Canny edge detection algorithm, we achieved precise segmentation of bottle bottoms, which are predominantly circular and distinct in their features.
  • Material Classification:
    • Initial attempts using optical properties and complex textural analyses like GLCM and LBP did not clearly distinguish between glass and plastic.
    • Success came when we shifted focus to shape-based differences:
      • Line Detection: For plastic bottles, which often feature bumps and valleys, the Hough Line Transformation identified characteristic lines, confirming the material as plastic.
      • Circle Detection: In the absence of lines, our attention turned to the outer circle of glass bottles known for a ring of ridges. Using adaptive thresholding and a customized Hough Circle Transform, we successfully identified these circular ridges indicative of glass.
